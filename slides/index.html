Latent Dynamics of Prediction-Market Price Paths

Learning latent repricing dynamics in terminally-absorbed price processes — non-parametric Bayesian and variational point-process models, with high-frequency Polymarket order-book data as the testbed.

Juan Mediavilla  ·  University College London
MSc Machine Learning
Academic Supervisor: Prof. Philip Treleaven
Technical Advisor: TBC (Bayesian time-series / point processes)

Research Motivations

The price path is a latent-structure learning problem: the hidden state that governs how a market reprices cannot be hand-specified, so it must be learned. Three structural peculiarities make prediction-market paths a distinct learning object — and one data gap kept the question closed.

  • Why machine learning, not econometrics. The data-generating process of repricing is unknown; rather than fix a parametric regime model in advance, the latent state, its cluster count, and the participant types are inferred from data — motivating non-parametric Bayesian and latent-variable methods.
  • Bounded & tradable as probability. Prediction markets price real-world events as continuously traded probabilities on (0,1); how those prices form at high frequency is poorly understood.
  • Terminal absorption at a known deadline. Every market is forced to an absorbing outcome ($0 or $1) at a roughly known deadline — a structure standard financial-dynamics models do not capture, and which the learned model must encode.
  • Observed terminal outcome & the data gap. Every path's realised result is recorded — per-path ground truth to validate the learned model. No public high-frequency order-book dataset of a prediction venue existed; I built one, removing the binding constraint on studying these dynamics.

Industry Background & Key Publications

Polymarket is the largest prediction-market venue (central limit order book, on-chain settlement, hundreds of markets resolved weekly); prior work established it clears near-efficiently gross of fees (1,348 markets, ~$445M), captured by a self-built 24/7 collector. The work sits against three literatures — prediction-market efficiency, the ML & point-process methods that are its foundation, and recent prediction-market microstructure.

Positioning. The point-process / latent-state ML literature is established and current — MMPP, Markov-modulated Hawkes, DP + VI on high-frequency financial data, now even applied to crypto and order flow. This thesis's novelty is the absorption-aware extension — latent intensity modulated by time-to-resolution toward a known 0/1 boundary — applied to prediction markets, a model class with no standard analogue that none of the above address.

Research Objectives

Three ML-method objectives — each justified by the same principle: the latent structure must be learned, not assumed — designed to hold whether the central signal is rich or null.

  • 1 · Learn the latent intensity state. Develop and fit an absorption-aware latent-variable model — a Markov-modulated Poisson process whose hidden intensity is modulated by time-to-resolution — by variational inference, with a self-exciting (Hawkes) generative model as the comparison.
  • 2 · Learn latent market & participant types. Apply non-parametric Bayesian clustering (Dirichlet-process mixtures, amortized VI) so the number and shape of latent types are inferred, over a hierarchical Bayesian backbone that pools across markets.
  • 3 · Build the dataset that makes learning possible. A reusable, gap-audited, millisecond-resolution L2 corpus and 24/7 pipeline — the testbed and the per-path ground truth for validating every learned model.

Research Methodology & Structure

A probe-and-gate arc: rule out the easy story, identify the real structure, then build the model that structure dictates — and validate it to the standard that retracted earlier findings.

Exp 1 — Supervised baselines & calibration (groundwork)
  • Supervised directional classifiers and calibration scoring establish the absence of an edge — the negative result that motivates the unsupervised turn to dynamics.
  • Five independent NO-EDGE findings; the market price beats every coarse prior tested.
Exp 2 — Unsupervised latent-state model selection
  • Learn the latent structure of price paths and select the model class.
  • Two pre-registered probes — falsify the Gaussian regime-switching (HMM) variance model, establish the latent intensity process that dictates the Exp 3 model.
Exp 3 — Absorption-aware latent-intensity model (the contribution)
  • Learn the intensity model and test its dependence on time-to-resolution; validate on held-out markets and realised outcomes.
  • Markov-modulated Poisson process with time-to-resolution modulation; variational inference; non-parametric Bayesian latent-type clustering.

Exp 1 · Supervised baselines & calibration — is the venue exploitable? Complete

Experiment
  • Test whether any supervised directional model or coarse prior beats the market.
  • Pre-registered, fee-aware backtests; market-clustered bootstrap CIs.
Data
  • 1,348 resolved markets, ~$445M notional.
  • 400 markets / 188,856 trades for the prior test.
  • On-chain maker reconstruction (100% matched).
Models — supervised / probabilistic
  • 4 directional classifiers; Brier-score calibration of priors vs the market.
  • Cross-market lead-lag; fee-aware PnL accounting.
  • Multiple-testing (BH) correction throughout.
Results
  • Five independent NO-EDGE results; near-efficient gross of fees.
  • Market Brier 0.063 beats every prior (0.18–0.25).
  • Conclusion: study price formation, not edge.

Exp 2 · Unsupervised latent-state model selection Complete

Experiment
  • Learn the latent-dynamics model class from the data.
  • Two pre-registered probes (v0 falsify, v1 establish).
Data
  • 10 liquid event markets; ~12.1M order-book delta rows.
  • Millisecond deltas reconstructed to mid-price.
  • Gap-audited; masked, never interpolated.
Models — latent-variable / unsupervised
  • v0: Gaussian regime-switching HMM on variance (falsified).
  • v1: jump-clustering + sticky-HMM + latent intensity.
  • Grid-invariance checks (1s / 10s / 60s).
Results
  • Variance-regime model FALSIFIED — regimes flicker (0/10).
  • Repricing intensity clustered & non-Poisson (10/10), CV 2.2–5.3.
  • Persistent hot/cold episodes (ρ=0.74, ~50 min memory).

Exp 3 · Absorption-aware latent-intensity model — MMPP + variational inference In progress

Experiment
  • Learn repricing intensity vs time-to-resolution τ — the core thesis contribution.
  • Endogenous self-excitation (Hawkes) vs exogenous regime (MMPP) as a real model-selection question.
Data
  • Clean server corpus + gold samples (resolve in-window).
  • ~10–15 complete lifecycles/day, accruing.
  • Event = repricing (>1 tick), price-level stratified; masked gaps as censored intervals.
Models — latent point process + inference
  • M0: homogeneous Poisson (null).
  • M1: MMPP latent hot/cold state — ESTABLISHED.
  • M2: + time-to-resolution τ (absorbing); variational inference; Hawkes comparison; non-parametric Bayesian (DP-mixture, amortized VI) latent types; hierarchical pooling, VI-vs-MCMC checked.
Results
  • H1 hot/cold states + dwell — ESTABLISHED (MMPP=M1).
  • H2 intensity vs τ is two-regime: reveal-before-wire markets freeze (intensity→0); sports markets spike at resolution.
  • H3 resolution as absorbing state (intensity→0) — M2b preferred.

Contributions to Science Academic

Methodological — the ML contribution

  • A new absorption-aware latent-intensity model class (MMPP modulated by time-to-resolution) for terminally-absorbed processes — no standard analogue.
  • Amortized variational inference (ELBO, inference networks) and a Dirichlet-process mixture to learn latent market and participant types — cluster count inferred, not assumed.
  • Hierarchical Bayesian pooling by Metropolis-within-Gibbs; VI-vs-MCMC cross-validation.
  • Hawkes vs MMPP framed as learned generative models of the event sequence.

Empirical

  • First order-book-resolution characterisation of prediction-market price dynamics.
  • Learns repricing-intensity clustering as the governing latent structure — not price-variance regimes.
  • On a novel, gap-audited dataset with complete path-to-resolution samples.
  • Falsifies the natural variance-regime model — a clean negative result.

  • Demonstrates rigorous, retraction-honest empirical practice end to end.
